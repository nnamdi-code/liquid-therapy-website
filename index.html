01 · The Gap
Work is intense.
Appreciation isn't.

Deadlines blur into evenings. Colleagues become co-survivors. And when the week finally ends, the moment of release — that first exhale on a Friday — goes completely unmarked.

Liquid Therapy™ exists in that gap. Not as a drink. As a format for acknowledging what just happened.

You don't buy a drink. You prescribe relief. You send it to someone who earned it. You open it when you've survived something worth surviving.

06 · POC Objectives
This is not
a tasting.

The office session is a validation exercise. We are not testing whether people enjoy the drinks. We are testing whether the concept works without explanation.

1 Do they get it immediately?
2 Do they think of someone to send it to?
3 Will they pay for it as a gift?
4 Which phrases land hardest?

Success on Thursday is not a full room. Success is three people who, without being asked, hold up a pouch and say "this is literally [colleague's name]."

The moment the concept travels — when someone describes it to a third person using their own words — that is when you know it works.

08 · Risks & How We Handle Them
Printing delays Clean white sticker labels are on-brand anyway. The phrase carries the concept, not the label quality. Ship it.
Low initial orders Convert face-to-face at the tasting before anyone leaves. A WhatsApp number at the table is worth ten Instagram followers.
Overcomplication One phrase does all the work. If you need to explain it, it hasn't landed. Trust the concept.
Logistics uncertainty Workers' Day is a public holiday — deliveries may need same-day or Sunday dispatch. Confirm delivery window in the pre-order message. Under-promise, over-deliver.
